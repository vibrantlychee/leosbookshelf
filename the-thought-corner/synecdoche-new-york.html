That Tiny Little Fear Called Reality

Thoughts on Kaufman's
Synecdoche: New York

By Leo Li; The Thought Corner

Kaufman’s postmodern Synecdoche: New York is horrific, perplexing, and memorable. Categorised by some as psychological horror, and others as pompous garbage, Kaufman, in proper postmodern fashion, attacks our very sense of existence through the worst fear known to humanity: reality...

悄悄的我走了，正如我悄悄的来；
我挥一挥衣袖，不带走一片云彩。

—徐志摩《再别康桥》
