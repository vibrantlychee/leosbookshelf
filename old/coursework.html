Memory-Efficient
Machine Learning
for
Dynamical Systems

Honours Thesis; Expected Completion by end of 2022

The rise of machine learning in recent times has led to major advancements in countless disciplines, including the study of dynamical systems. The forecasting of dynamical systems is inherently a time-series prediction task, and so it is no surprise that machine learning techniques such as recurrent neural networks and kernel methods have become widely used for the learning and predicting of dynamical systems. In particular, memory-efficiency is one of the main concerns due to the large scale data generated by dynamical systems, with random feature maps emerging as a highly favoured model due to its reduction to a linear regression problem. Reservoir computing is an implementation of a RNN that has also shown promise for memory-efficient prediction of dynamical systems. This paper seeks to compare the two methods to identify the optimal memory management method when learning the time-t map of a dynamical system for forecasting.
